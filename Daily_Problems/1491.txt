Problem in Simple Words

You are given salaries of employees.
You must:

Remove the lowest salary

Remove the highest salary

Find the average of the remaining salaries

Key Idea

Instead of sorting the array, we can do everything in one loop:

Find minimum salary

Find maximum salary

Find total sum

Then subtract the minimum and maximum from the sum and divide by the remaining count.

What Each Variable Means

min ‚Üí stores the smallest salary

max ‚Üí stores the largest salary

sum ‚Üí stores the sum of all salaries

Step-by-Step Working
Step 1: Initialize values

Assume the first salary is both minimum and maximum initially.

Example:
salary = [4000, 3000, 1000, 2000]

min = 4000
max = 4000
sum = 0

Step 2: Loop through salaries

For every salary:

Add it to sum

Update min if current salary is smaller

Update max if current salary is larger

After loop:

sum = 10000
min = 1000
max = 4000

Step 3: Remove min and max from sum
sum - min - max = 10000 - 1000 - 4000 = 5000

Step 4: Divide by remaining employees

We removed 2 salaries (min & max):

remaining employees = salary.length - 2
= 4 - 2 = 2

Step 5: Final average
average = 5000 / 2 = 2500.0

Why We Use (double)

Java divides integers as integers.
Casting to double ensures we get decimal precision.

Final Formula to Remember (Exam Gold ‚ú®)
Average = (Total Sum ‚àí Min ‚àí Max) / (n ‚àí 2)

Time & Space Complexity (Exam Ready)

Time Complexity: O(n)
(Single pass through array)

Space Complexity: O(1)
(No extra data structures used)
